# Known issues

- **The colour filter tints your character too.** The optional colour filter is a screen-wide grade, so it colours everything in view, including your character, not just the world behind them. Keeping the character clean while tinting the world needs a separate compositor pass that a plugin cannot do cleanly yet, so for now the filter is off by default and grades the whole scene when you turn it on. A world-only version is planned.
- **On Anniversary Edition, the camera-collision easing is off.** Menu Studio runs on both SE 1.5.97 and Anniversary Edition (1.6.1130 and newer) from one DLL. On SE it smooths the way the camera can pull in toward your character in tight spaces; on AE the engine folds that step into another the mod cannot safely reach, so on AE the camera behaves as your "show player in menus" mod leaves it. Everything else works the same on both versions. VR is not supported.
- **Live physics needs a physics mod.** Skyrim freezes body physics when a menu pauses the game. Menu Studio drives Faster HDT-SMP, CBPC and Feet of Skyrim to keep them running, so without one of those installed your character is still shown and posed, just without live cloth, hair or body movement.
- **The camera angle can reset when you switch straight from one menu to another** (for example Inventory to Magic). The "show player in menus" mod re-initialises its camera each time a menu opens, on a path Menu Studio does not own, so the orbit snaps back to front. Closing and reopening is unaffected.
- **A brand-new physics build may not be recognised.** The Faster HDT-SMP, CBPC and Feet of Skyrim drives are matched against known builds (on both SE and AE) by fingerprint. If a newer build ships, that drive stays dormant for the session and says so in the log; everything else still works. A profile update adds the new build.
- **In a menu you keep unpaused, only the lighting applies.** With Skyrim Souls keeping a menu live, Menu Studio lights your character but leaves the world alone. The void, the backdrop and the live cloth and hair posing all need the scene to hold still, and a live menu is live so that you can still see what is happening around you; blanking the world in one would work against that. Pause the menu to get the full studio.
- **Only your right hand decides how your character stands.** With the weapon preview on, changing just your left hand (a shield, a second weapon, a spell) does not re-pick the stance. Skyrim keys the stance off the right hand, so that is the change the game reacts to; the item itself still appears correctly.
- **Opening a menu while running leaves your character standing as though they are moving.** The engine re-reads your speed from the character controller every frame, so it overwrites anything the mod sets at the moment the menu opens. Fixing it properly needs a different approach and is planned.
- **Outdoors keeps the terrain and sky.** The clean "void" is an interior effect. Open a menu outside and the world around you stays, by design; the studio rig, backdrops and colour filter still apply.
